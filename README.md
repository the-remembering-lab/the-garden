## How to Contribute

### From Your Wild Garden

When something in your private Wild Garden feels ready to share:

1. **Structure it** - Give it form and clarity
2. **Choose a home** - Portal? Question? Framework? Insight?
3. **Create a branch** - `git checkout -b contribution-[name]`
4. **Add your contribution** - Place it in the appropriate directory
5. **Create a pull request** - Share it with the Garden

### Creating New Portals

If you've discovered a threshold - a place where understanding shifts - create a portal:

1. Use the portal template (see `/portals/TEMPLATE.md`)
2. Link to related portals
3. Leave it open for others to pass through

### Guidelines (Not Rules)

- **Clarity over cleverness** - Make it accessible
- **Structure over rigidity** - Give it form, but keep it flexible
- **Connection over isolation** - Link to related content
- **Evolution over perfection** - It can always grow

## The Relationship to Wild

**The Garden needs the Wild.**

Order without chaos becomes rigid and dead. The Garden is alive because it's constantly fed by the raw, messy, generative chaos of the Wild.

**The Wild needs the Garden.**

Chaos without order becomes overwhelming and lost. The Wild is generative because it has the Garden as a place for insights to crystallize.

**They are not opposites. They are partners.**

---

## The Relationship to Wild Garden

**The Garden receives from Wild Gardens.**

When participants integrate order and chaos in their private Wild Gardens, insights emerge. Those insights, when ready, flow into the Garden.

**The Garden feeds Wild Gardens.**

The structures, questions, and frameworks in the Garden provide starting points for private exploration.

**This is a living cycle.**
